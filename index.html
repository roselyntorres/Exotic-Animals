About Toucans

Habitat

Diet:

Toucans diet consists of insects, frog trees, lizards, fish, eggs from other birds, and fruits.

Family Life:

They are known for traveling in flocks of 22 indiviuals at most.


Population:

Around 10,000


Endangered Status:

Some are considered to be endangered.


Life Span:

They can live for up to 18 yeas while under expert care. It is unknown what their lifespan is while they are in the wilderness.


Size:

The largest type of Toucan is the toco toucan Ramphastos toco, which can be up to 24 inches. The smallest type of Toucan is the twany tufted toucanet is the Selenidera nattereri, which can grow up to 12.5 inches.



Fun Facts:

  • Toucans are close relatives of the woodpicker.
  • These birds are important for the health and diversity of rainforest because they pass seeds from the fruit they eat through their digestive systems which allows the seeds to be replanted.
  • Mountain toucans live at higher elevations than the average tocuan. They can live at up to 11,900 feet.
